My Mood Alexa Application

User Manual

System Purpose

My Mood is an interactive Alexa application that a user can talk about their day with. The goal of this application is to improve, assess, and get help for the user's mental state.

Depression not only has an impact on the individual person, it also has a great impact on the society around them. Unfortunately, depressed individuals tend to have limited knowledge and access to resources regarding their disorder.

The system provides tools for potentially depressed users, including:

  1. Depression alleviation by telling Alexa about their day
  2. Mental health diagnosis by taking an assessment based on the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders
  3. Location-based suggestion for professional help
The ultimate goal of the system is to give users more knowledge of the available resources that can help their mental state. Submitted research data would be towards a good cause and simultaneously improve the system. By doing so, we hope users will become more aware of their depression by encouraging them be more proactive in curing their depression.

Installation

  1. Make sure your Amazon Echo is linked to your Amazon account.
  2. Sign in to your Amazon account on a web browser.
  3. Navigate to the Alexa Skills page here.
  4. Search for "My Mood" in the search bar
  5. Select "My Mood", which opens the skill detail page
  6. Click the "Enable" option
  7. Access skill by using the keyword "Open My Mood" on the Amazon Echo.

How to activate each feature

MyMood has three main features:
To open My Mood, say "Alexa, open my mood."
  1. To converse with Alexa, start with the keyword Today. Ex: "Today, it's my birthday."
    • MyMood will respond with congratulatory or sympathetic statement based on classification.
  2. To have Alexa assess your mental health, say, "Alexa, ask My Mood to take an assessment."
    • Follow assessment directions and answer questions with "yes" or "no".
    • Upon completion of the assessment, MyMood will give you a possible diagnosis for your condition.
  3. To request information about nearby professional help, say, "Alexa, ask My Mood to look for professional help."
    • To ensure proper functionality, enable location accessing permissions on your Alexa.
    • MyMood will locate a nearby mental health institute and provide their information based the location set on your Alexa.
  • To hear your options, say "help".
  • To repeat what Alexa said, say "repeat that".
  • To stop Alexa, say "Alexa, stop".

MyMood Application FAQ

Q: Why does Alexa sometimes take long to respond?
 A: It did not hear you or it is processing the response.
Q: Why does Alexa shut off midway?
 A: Alexa requires you to respond within 8 seconds.
Q: Why does Alexa sometimes respond sympathetic to a positive statement or vice versa?
 A: The machine learning model's accuracy is not perfect.
Q: Alexa diagnosed me with a very secure case of depression, but I don't think it's right.
 A: The system is not a sure measure for depression. See a mental health professional to be sure.
Q: Alexa is not getting my location.
 A: Make sure it is connected to the internet and location accessing permissions are enabled.
Q: I have a problem not mentioned here.
 A: Email us at mymood491@gmail.com


My Mood Database Interface

How to Use Our Database

  1. Login to our database with valid credentials.
  2. Having granted access, you can view a table of available shared sessions between users.
      • If you have trouble finding specific information, use the search bar and filter options to search for a specific session, user, time, or word in a response.
  3. To view a session's responses, click on the row of that session that you wish to view.
  4. Viewing a session will provide the responses of the user throughout the session.

Attributes

Session ID: Identifier that uniquely identifies the session between the user and Alexa.
User ID: Identifier that uniquely identifies the user for a specific session.
Response: The statement recorded by the user or Alexa.
Time: The time that was recorded when a user started a session.

MyMood Database Interface FAQ
